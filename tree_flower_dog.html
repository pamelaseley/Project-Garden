Project Garden

  • Lettuce

    Lettuce is a cool season crop that grows best in the spring and fall, but can be grown year round in mild climates.

  • Spinach

    Spinach is a cool season crop that grows best in the spring and fall, but can be grown year round in mild climates.

  • Tomatoes

    Tomatoes are a warm season crop and should be seeded indoors in late winter or early spring and transplanted out after last frost.

  • Cucumbers

    Cucumbers are a warm season crop and should be seeded or transplanted out after last frost. They will need to be provided with a trellis or other form of support.

  • Squash

    Squash is a warm season crop and should be seeded or transplanted out after last frost. They can be planted in hills or rows.

  • Peppers

    Peppers are also a warm season crop and should be started indoors several weeks before last frost. Transplant out after last frost.

  • Carrots

    Carrots are a cool season crop and should be seeded outdoors in well-draining soil as soon as the ground can be worked in the spring.

  • Beans

    Beans are a warm season crop and should be seeded or transplanted out after last frost. They will need to be provided with a trellis or other form of support.

  • Radishes

    Radishes are a cool season crop and should be seeded outdoors in well-draining soil as soon as the ground can be worked in the spring.

  • Corn

    Corn is a warm season crop and should be seeded or transplanted out after last frost. Plant in blocks to ensure good pollination.
